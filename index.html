Oscar Predictions

Exploring the influencing factors relating to the winners of the Oscar's Best Picture award using computer algorithms.

Brooks Lobe, Graham Kelly, Kimiko Farmer, William Menten-Weil

Read About Our Project

What data is tied to winning Best Picture?

Our group sought to investigate the differences that lie between human and machine prediction methodologies. In order to explore this subject, we tasked A) Computer/statistical algorithms and B) Human survey participants with defining what factors correlate most strongly with movies that win the Oscar for Best Picture.

The factors provided were as follows: Average Critic Score (Rotten Tomatoes), Average User Score (Rotten Tomatoes), Budget, IMDB Rating, Number of IMDB Ratings, Producer, Profit Ratio, and Total Box Office Sales.

In order to provide our algorithms with the necessary information, our group performed web scraping on sites like Rotten Tomatoes, putting together data which we fed to our models.

What We Discovered

So far, our computer models have yet to produce statistically significant results. We are in the process of developing upon our methods in order to create some interesting insights!

We are also eagerly looking into comparing our algorithmic results against the results of a survey we ran, where we asked participants to perform the same ranking that we are asking of our algorithm.
